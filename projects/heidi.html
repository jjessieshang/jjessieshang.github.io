Problem: Prehospital telemedicine triage systems combined with machine learning methods have the potential to improve accuracy and safely redirect low-acuity patients from attending the emergency department (ED). However, research in prehospital settings is limited while ED overcrowding and adverse patient outcomes are common.

Solution:
In this scoping review, we sought to characterize the current state of ML-enhanced methods applied to prehospital telemedicine emergency triage without the use of physical assessments. Overall, the most popular ML algorithm was support vector machine (SVM; n=8, 53%) and tree-based methods, including random forest (RF; n=6, 40%) and extreme gradient boosting (XGBoost; n=4, 27%). Other popular algorithms were neural networks (NN; n=5, 33%), with various implementations observed, such as deep NNs, bidirectional long short-term memory models, and ensemble of deep learning networks.
In addition to identifying 11 key predictors to the remote pre-hospital triage process, we present analysis of subjectivity in ML systems and underscore the need for nuance regarding objectivity and ground truth.
